So, what's changed?
For those of you that are familiar with the previous iteration of the submitter there are some subtle but significant differences.
We had three major goals for this new release.
The first goal was to greatly increase support for Solaris and USD.
The second was to simplify the submitter.
And the third was to make the submitter more deterministic and less of a black-box.
I’ll dive into the details of the first goal when we take a look at the submitters.
To achieve the second goal, we decided to create multiple submitter types.
We now have three; Solaris, Husk and ROPs.
But we’re not stopping there - we are currently working on more.
By focusing each submitter type to a specific workflow, we’ve been able to improve the user experience so that artists are only presented with parameters and choices that are relevant to them.
To achieve the third goal, we’ve decided to establish a 1:1 relationship between a Conductor submitter and a job.
With the previous iteration, you were able to submit multiple jobs from a single Conductor submitter.
While at times this was a handy convenience it also created issues where users did not have visibility on how certain parameters in the submitter would affect all their jobs.
By establishing a 1:1 relationship, the user gains greater visibility on how an individual job will be submitted.
We’re also hard at work at writing some tools to make it a lot more convenient to submit multiple jobs.
Look for those in a future release.
